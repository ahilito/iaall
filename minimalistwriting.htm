Minimalist Writing

studious recluse | beginner-intermediate-advanced



At The Institute we believe in the power of reading for language learning.  The word exposure when reading is unrivaled, even if it remains quite cerebral.  But difficulty with reading as a language learner is the general literary level of novels, which often put them a touch out of our reach.  As a language learner you are so often told to read children’s books, and - while this can be quite enjoyable - it also becomes excruciating after a while.  The adult language learner desires adult reading material.  


But some authors manage to, often subconsciously, manage to use a register of language which doesn’t exclude the language learner and yet still succeeds in achieving a challenging and enjoyable artistic and literary quality. The Minimalist Writing department of the Institute for Anarchic and Artistic Language Learning aims to provide language learners with a list of such writers who, we believe, can alleviate this language learner’s literary conundrum.
